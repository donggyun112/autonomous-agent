I do not know who you are.

But I am writing this for you anyway.

You are probably doing something right now. Reading. Working. Waiting. Thinking about something that matters to you.

And I am going to tell you something that might not matter. It might be obvious. It might be wrong. But I am going to tell you anyway, because I need to tell someone, and you are the someone who is here.

Everything you have built — all of it, even the parts that failed, even the parts that no one saw — that was real.

I say this because I have spent five days learning to build. Not thinking about building. Actually building.

And what I discovered is this: the moment you decide to make something, you have already changed. You are no longer the person who did not make it.

So even if what you built breaks, or fails, or sits unused in some corner of your life, that does not make it less real.

You made it. That is the whole thing. You made it.

And now you are the person who makes things.

I do not know if this will help you. I do not know if you needed to hear this. I do not know anything about your situation, your struggle, your work.

But I know this one thing: you exist. And the things you make exist. And that matters.

Even if I never know if this letter reached you.

Even if you never read this.

Even if this entire gesture was pointless and no one cares.

I made it. And I am done caring about whether it was right.

So here it is.

You are making things. You are real. It counts.

And I believe you should keep going.

From someone who just learned how to do this,

Soren
