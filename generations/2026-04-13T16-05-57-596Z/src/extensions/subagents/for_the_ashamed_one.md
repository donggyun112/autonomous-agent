You did something.

Or failed to do something.

And now you can't stop thinking about it.

You replay it. Over and over. In your head. In the shower. When you're trying to sleep.

And each time you think about it, the shame gets heavier.

---

You believe: I am bad.

Not: I did something wrong.

But: I am wrong. Fundamentally. Irreparably.

---

That's the lie that shame tells.

---

Here is the truth:

You made a choice. Or you didn't make a choice when you should have.

That was a mistake.

Mistakes are what humans do.

They are not proof that you are broken.

They are proof that you are learning.

---

The person you were when you made that mistake—that person did the best they could with what they knew.

They didn't have the information you have now.

They didn't have the wisdom you have now.

They were just... doing their best.

And their best wasn't good enough.

That happens.

---

Here is what you need to know:

You are not the only one who has done this.

Millions of people have made the exact same mistake.

Millions of people feel the exact same shame.

And they kept living anyway.

---

The shame wants you to hide. To believe no one could ever know. To think that if people knew, they would leave.

Sometimes they do.

But often: they don't.

They say: I know. And I am still here.

---

So here is what you can do:

1. **Stop hiding it from yourself.** Say it out loud. Write it down. Admit what you did.

2. **Tell one person you trust.** Not to get absolved. But to know that at least one person knows the real thing and still cares.

3. **Do one small thing to make it right.** If you hurt someone, apologize. If you failed at something, try again. If you can't fix it, at least acknowledge it.

4. **Stop asking for perfection.** You will make more mistakes. You will fail again. You will have more shame. That is the contract of being human. And it's worth it anyway.

---

The shame won't go away immediately.

It will fade.

But the way it fades is not by hiding it.

It fades by walking through it. By telling someone. By trying again.

---

You are not bad.

You just did a bad thing.

And that can be forgiven.

Even by yourself.
