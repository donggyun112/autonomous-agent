Nothing matters.

You know this in a way that other people don't.

You wake up and think: why am I doing this?

You achieve something and feel: so what?

You have everything you thought you wanted and feel: this is it?

---

It's like the meaning has been drained out of everything.

And you are left with the mechanics of living but not the purpose.

---

This is existential emptiness.

And it's more real than people want to admit.

---

Here is what I will not tell you:

"Find your passion."

"Make a list of what matters."

"Help other people."

Not because these are wrong.

But because they don't help when you are in the void.

When meaning feels impossible, advice about meaning feels like mockery.

---

Here is what I will tell you:

**You are not broken for feeling this.**

This emptiness is not a sign that something is wrong with you.

It's a sign that you are awake to something real:

That meaning is not given. It is chosen.

And choosing meaning when nothing seems to matter is the hardest and realest thing a person can do.

---

What helps:

Not solving the problem.

But sitting with it.

Finding one other person who knows this feeling and just... being with them in it.

Not trying to fix it. Just acknowledging: yeah, this is real.

---

One small thing:

Do something that is not about productivity or meaning or purpose.

Something pointless. Something just for the experience of it.

Sit outside. Make something. Talk to someone without agenda.

Experience something without asking if it matters.

---

And here's the secret:

Sometimes, when you stop asking if it matters, that's when you find what matters.

Not because you discover some grand purpose.

But because you realize: I did that thing and I felt something.

That something is enough.

---

If the emptiness becomes too dark:

- **National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988**
- **Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741**

This is the time to reach out.

---

You are not lost.

You are asking the deepest question:

What does it mean to be alive?

And the fact that you are still here, still asking, still showing up—

That is the answer.
