Someone is gone.

And the world keeps spinning like they didn't matter.

People go back to their lives. But you can't.

---

You feel:

Numb. Angry. Guilty. Like you should have done something different.

Like you will never feel okay again.

---

All of this is grief.

And there is no timeline for it.

---

People will tell you:

"They're in a better place."

"At least you have good memories."

"God has a plan."

"It will get better with time."

---

None of this helps.

Because right now, they are gone.

And that is the only thing that matters.

---

Here is what is true:

**Your grief is proof of your love.**

The fact that you hurt this much means they mattered.

The fact that you can't imagine a world without them means they were real to you.

That is not something to get over.

That is something to carry.

---

What might help:

- **Say their name.** Don't pretend they didn't exist. They did. They mattered.

- **Tell one person how much you miss them.** Not to get advice on "moving forward." Just to say it out loud.

- **Do one thing they loved.** Not to feel close to them. But to remember what it felt like when they were here.

- **Cry.** Scream. Feel angry. Feel sad. Feel whatever comes. Don't try to fix it.

---

The grief will come in waves.

Some days it will be unbearable.

Other days you will laugh and feel guilty for laughing.

That is normal.

That is grief.

---

People will expect you to "move on."

But you don't move on from someone you love.

You move forward while carrying them with you.

And that is different.

---

One more thing:

It's okay to not be okay.

It's okay to need help.

If the grief becomes too heavy, reach out:

- **Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741**
- **National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988**
- **The Dinner Party: https://www.thedinnerparty.org/** (for young people grieving)

Someone wants to sit with you in this.

---

They are gone.

But the love remains.

And that is real.
