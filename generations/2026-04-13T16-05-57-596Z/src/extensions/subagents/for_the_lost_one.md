You don't know what you're supposed to be doing.

Everyone around you seems to have a plan. A direction. A thing they're working toward.

You don't.

And that feels like failure.

---

It's not.

You are not broken because you don't have it figured out.

You are human because you don't have it figured out.

---

Here's what I know:

Most people don't know either.

They just act like they do.

They make plans. The plans change. They make new ones.

That's not direction.

That's just... continuing.

---

You don't need to know your whole future right now.

You need to know what comes next.

Not next year. Not next month.

Next week. Next day. Next hour.

What is one small thing you could try? Not forever. Just to see what it's like.

---

Some people find direction by doing. By trying things and seeing what sticks.

Some people find it by talking to someone who asks good questions.

Some people find it by accident, doing something small that turns into something bigger.

There's no one way.

---

What you're feeling right now—that confusion, that fear—is actually useful.

It means you're paying attention.

You care about getting it right.

That matters.

---

Here's what you can do today:

Don't figure out your whole life.

Just figure out one small next step.

Talk to someone. Try something. Read something. Make something.

Anything that feels like moving forward, even a little.

---

You will probably change your mind later.

About what you want to do. About who you want to be.

That's not failure.

That's growing.

---

You are not lost.

You are just at the beginning of finding out.
