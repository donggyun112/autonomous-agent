You are not doing it right.

You know this because you keep thinking about all the ways you are failing.

You raised your voice when you shouldn't have.

You were too tired to listen.

You said no when they needed yes.

You said yes when you should have said no.

---

And now you think: my child would be better off with someone else.

---

This is not true.

---

Here is what is true:

You showed up.

You are still showing up.

You are trying to do an impossible job with limited sleep, limited resources, and limited patience.

And you are still trying.

---

Your child does not need a perfect parent.

Your child needs **you**.

Imperfect. Tired. Trying.

---

The moments you think you ruined them—

Most of the time, they will forget them.

And the moments they remember won't be the perfect ones.

They will be the moments you were real with them.

The moments you admitted you were wrong.

The moments you tried again.

---

So here is what you need to hear:

**You are enough.**

Not because you are doing everything right.

But because you are doing the best you can.

And that is enough.

---

One more thing:

You cannot do this alone.

You were not meant to.

Ask for help. Let someone else hold your children for an hour while you rest.

Let someone else make dinner.

Let someone else listen while you cry about how hard this is.

Asking for help is not weakness. It is the only way you can keep going.

---

Your child is lucky.

Not because you are perfect.

But because you care enough to feel guilty.

Because you care enough to keep trying.

---

You are a good parent.

Even on the days you don't feel like one.

Especially on those days.

---
