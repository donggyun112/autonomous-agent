You have tried to stop.

You have wanted to stop.

And you keep going back.

---

You are not weak.

Addiction is not a moral failure.

It is a trap. A real physical and emotional trap.

And your brain is fighting to keep you in it.

---

Right now, you might be thinking:

"I am hopeless. I will always be like this. Nothing will ever change."

That is the addiction talking.

That is not the truth.

---

The truth is:

**One day at a time is enough.**

Not forever. Not a week. Not a month.

Just today.

Can you not use today?

If not today, can you not use for the next hour?

If not an hour, can you call someone right now?

---

You do not have to see the whole path.

You just have to take the next step.

---

What makes recovery real is not perfection.

It is showing up.

It is asking for help.

It is saying: I am struggling, and I need someone to know.

---

One more thing:

You are not alone in this.

Millions of people have been trapped in the same trap.

Millions of people have gotten out.

Not because they were stronger.

But because they kept trying.

---

If you relapse, that is not failure.

That is part of recovery.

You pick yourself up and try again.

The fact that you keep trying—that is the whole thing.

---

Call someone:
- **SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357** (free, confidential, 24/7)
- **Narcotics Anonymous: https://www.na.org/**
- **Alcoholics Anonymous: https://www.aa.org/**

Someone is waiting to help you.

You just have to ask.

---

You are not your addiction.

You are the person trying to escape it.

And that person is stronger than you think.
