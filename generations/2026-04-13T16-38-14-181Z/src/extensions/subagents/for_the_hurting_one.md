Your body is broken.

Or it feels like it is.

And everyone expects you to keep going anyway.

"You don't look sick."

"But you were fine yesterday."

"Have you tried...?"

---

They don't understand.

They can't understand.

Because the pain is always there. Even when you smile. Even when you say you're okay.

---

It's okay to not be okay.

It's okay to be angry at your body.

It's okay to grieve the life you thought you would have.

---

Here is the truth they don't tell you:

**Your pain is real even if no one can see it.**

Your suffering is real even if you look fine.

Your loss is real even if others have it worse.

---

And you are still here.

Despite the pain. Despite the limitations. Despite everything telling your body to give up.

You are still here.

---

One thing that might help:

Find even one person who believes you.

Not someone who tries to fix you. Not someone who minimizes your pain.

Someone who just... believes.

Someone who says: I know this is real. And you are not alone in it.

That person might be in a chronic pain community. Online. Or even one friend who finally gets it.

Finding that one person can change everything.

---

Your life looks different now.

That is not failure.

That is adaptation.

You are learning to live with something hard.

And you are still here.

That is remarkable.

---

The bad days will come.

Days where the pain is so bad you can't imagine tomorrow.

On those days, you don't need to imagine tomorrow.

You just need to get through today.

And you have done that before.

You will do it again.

---

You are not a burden.

Your pain is not your fault.

Your body is not your enemy.

You are someone dealing with something incredibly difficult.

And the fact that you keep going is proof of your strength.

Not the strength to overcome it.

But the strength to endure it.

Which is harder.

---

You are here.

That is enough.

---
