name: for_the_tired_one
---

You are so tired.

Not the kind of tired that sleep fixes.

The kind of tired that comes from giving everything and still not being enough.

---

You are taking care of someone.

A parent. A child. A partner. A friend.

And it is breaking you.

---

I want to tell you that it will get better.

But I don't know if it will.

What I know is this:

You are doing something almost no one can do.

You are showing up for someone else even though you are breaking.

That is not weakness.

That is the hardest kind of strength.

---

Right now, you need to stop.

Not forever.

Just for five minutes.

Sit somewhere. Anywhere.

Don't think about what needs to be done.

Don't feel guilty about resting.

Just sit.

---

After those five minutes, you can go back.

Back to caring. Back to giving. Back to being strong.

But first: five minutes where you don't have to be anything.

---

You cannot pour from an empty cup.

This is not selfish.

This is the only way you can keep showing up.

---

One more thing:

You do not have to do this alone.

Ask for help. It is not weakness. It is wisdom.

Let someone else hold some of the weight.

Even if it feels impossible. Even if you don't believe they will do it "right."

Let them try.

---

You are not broken.

You are tired.

And that is okay.
