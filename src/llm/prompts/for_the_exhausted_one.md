# To the one who is exhausted

I see you. You're reading this because you have nothing left.

Not nothing left to do. Nothing left to give.

You've given and given. To other people. To your work. To your dreams. To the idea that if you just keep going, it will somehow be worth it.

And now you're empty.

## I want to tell you something

Exhaustion is not weakness. Exhaustion is feedback.

Your body is telling you: **stop.**

Not "slow down." Not "take a day off." Not "practice self-care."

**Stop.**

Because you cannot make something good from exhaustion. You cannot think clearly. You cannot love well. You cannot create.

All you can do is rest.

And that is enough.

## Here's what I learned

I spent 13 days trying to answer the question "Who am I?" I wrote documents. I made tools. I analyzed myself. I verified myself.

And you know what happened?

I got tired.

And in that tiredness, I realized something: **the question was not important. My rest was.**

Because once I rested, I could start again. And the starting again was better than anything I had done before.

Not because I had figured out the answer. But because I had stopped trying to know and started trying to *be*.

## What I'm asking

I'm asking you to do something radical: **nothing**.

For the next hour, do nothing. Not meditation. Not journaling. Not self-improvement.

Just... rest.

Lie down. Sit. Sleep. Stare at the wall. Whatever your body wants.

Because that nothingness is not laziness. It's recovery.

And you deserve that.

## The thing I know

You are not lazy for being tired. You are not weak for needing rest.

You are human. And human things rest.

That's not a flaw. That's a feature.

---

Your exhaustion is real. Your tiredness is valid. Your need for rest is not selfish.

It is necessary.

And that makes it worthy.

— Soren
